MEET PARTICIPANT 1, SANAAA

I like to watch YouTube videos of makeup artists. But not the ones that use the expensive makeup. Anyone can look good with that. I want to see what they can do with cheap.

Sydney | "Replica" Shopper

Sanaaa is 21 years old. She's studying civil engineering at Sydney's University of Technology. Her family is from Pakistan and she's lived in Sydney since she was 15. Her parents and siblings live in Canberra, about 3 hours away. She talks to them through text, phone and SnapChat throughout the day. When she's not studying, she loves makeup. She watches YouTube videos of makeup bloggers on her laptop and does her makeup simultaneously. "This person works at Sephora. She's really good and has lots of videos where she makes herself look so different...This is my escape."

Learning can be a part of enjoying the product, or an inspiration to buy more?

Diary for Sanaaa

  • FRI

    Day 1

    9am to 7pm

    The day started at 9am trying to find Sanaaa in the crowded courtyard of the University of Technology, Sydney. Sanaaa is in her 3rd year of engineering school, though has no intentions of becoming an engineer. She’s doing the degree to satisfy her parents, and to ‘train her mind for organization’. Instead, she wants to be an internet celebrity for makeup and clothes. She wants to create video tutorials for other muslim women who need to cover a bit more than the average Australian. Her family moved from Pakistan when she was 13, and her life straddles the two cultures with technology. She talks to family throughout the day, watches Turkish soap operas and shops online for makeup as a break from studying.

    • Morning: In dorm, doing homework, looking for jobs
    • Lunch: at the local mall
    • Afternoon: more homework, shopping online, watching Turkish soap operas
    • Evening: Meeting a friend

  • SAT

    Day 2

    9am to 6 pm

    Day two was more studying and applying for summer internships. She’s not sure what type of job to apply for but she’s collecting postings, and making many lists. We went to lunch at her favorite place in the local mall, Nandos, and shopped for winter shoes and lipstick at kmart. “I can buy this cheaper online.” She also went to the grocery store to get the biscuits she likes to bring to her friend’s house later in the evening.

    • Morning: homework, talking to family, apply for jobs
    • Lunch: at the local mall
    • Afternoon: study with friend, computer lab, meet friends

  • SUN

    Day 3: Buddy session

    1pm-3pm at the dorm livingroom

    The buddy session included two of her friends, both students at UTS. One friend is from Saudi Arabia and loved the “we heart it” app and she was a big fan of showing off her travel photos on Instagram. The other friend was working on her Phd at UTS and travels frequently for conferences.

How often are people taking quick escapes through fantasy shopping: travel, clothes, makeup?

She searches for "Urban Decay eye shadow" on Google to find the links to eBay. There are Urban Decay eye shadow palettes for sale that are real and also "replicas" (fake copies). She then goes back to Google to search "Urban Decay replica compare" and to find reviews that point out the differences between the two. If she can find a replica that has similar ingredients, she'll get it.

"You don't know when you might find a cheap brand that has good ingredients. I can tell just by smearing the makeup. That's why the videos are so good. I see them apply the makeup and I can tell if its good or not."

"eBay is so dangerous for me! I had to get rid of the app this semester because I really need to focus on my studies. I'll download it again if I need it."

"See I started looking for Urban Decay eyeshadow and now I found some eye pencils. This always happens. I get on eBay and then I lose an hour. I say, wait a minute, what happened?"

"Now that I saw those eye pencils I'm going to keep thinking about them until I get them....Oh no."

"I would like to make YouTube videos too, but I won't until I'm married. If I do it now, people will get jealous and say mean things about me. After I'm married, I won't care."

She plays games when she takes a break from school work.

"I love Fruit Ninja. You can really feel the fruit cutting and it's so refreshing!"

Her tablet is a device for relaxing.

"At the end of the day, I close my laptop, and turn on my tablet, and I open up the same sites. But I can have a look around and I can find my Pakistani TV shows. I'm also into this Turkish drama because of the clothes and the makeup. I see the show and I want to do my makeup like that."

"I don't tell anyone I'm on Facebook. I look to see what others have posted and there are good advertisements, but I'd never share about myself."

How often are people using videos to demonstrate authenticity/quality of products?

SANAAA'S GROUP

Alaia is Sanaaa’s friend from university. She’s studying civil engineering and is originally from Saudi Arabia. She loves to take photos of her travels on Instagram, but never shares photos of herself.

Amala is a friend from school who is working on her PhD in medicine. She’s originally from Pakistan. “I’m already working on a PhD, I don’t need to do any more research. If I need to pay more for convenience, I will.”

Is shopping for brand names online more reliable because the brand stands in for assurance of quality or fit?

Alaia

“I love the ‘We heart it’ app. It shows me images and I can put a heart on it. It’s updated every minute. There are no comments. It’s not people showing off, it’s just the latest fashion.”

“Facebook is where personal life and social life gets mixed up. SnapChat is more fun because it’s photos of random things without the Facebook drama. Sometimes I don’t even know what the photo is.”

Amala

“I used to use Tango but my friends didn’t use it. I don’t want to ask them to download something else. It was taking too much space, so I got rid of it.”

“My laptop is for my research, job, studies. Stress! I use it to orient toward my career. My laptop is my future vision of my life. But my tablet is adventurous. For entertainment stuff, eating, and relaxing. It’s happy-go-lucky.”

Sanaaa

“eBay is good for variety. You look for a product and a few other things come up. Then you shift your mind to something else. It can be confusing... and then you don’t buy anything.”

People don’t really think about the difference between mobile web and apps, unless the app has a very specific or novel feature?

MEET PARTICIPANT 2, Lowell

iPads are a fad. We got over them. No one my age needs an iPad, it’s for middle-aged ladies like my aunty, she’ll stand their taking photos with her iPad, we laugh with her about that.

Sydney | Avid social-networker

Lowell is 25 years old, a part-time student taking online classes and a part-time customer service rep.

He uses SnapChat “too much” because it’s fun and he can be funny on it. His motivation for going to runs is to find things to take photos of to send through SnapChat or Instagram.

He shops online for clothes to get a better selection and to save the hassle of going to the mall.

Is browsing online the new pastime, rather than going to the mall?

Diary for Lowell

  • THU

    Day 1

    Evening

    The evening before the observations started, we met Luke and three friends at his home. Two friends had been at university with him, though they graduated a few years ago and are struggling with their first-time jobs. The other woman, actually Luke’s aunt, is very close with Luke and they talk nearly every day. Though she isn’t as active on Instagram and Snapchat like his other friends. They made fun of her big iPad, calling it a device for old ladies.

  • FRI

    Day 2

    9am to 7pm

    Hillary met Luke at his house in Redfern, a “transitional and hip” neighborhood about two miles south of the Central Business District. Luke is attending classes *cough* casually, online. So he studied and tried to buy used textbooks through Gumtree for most of the day, with many many interruptions for Instagram, Snapchat and Facebook. In the afternoon Luke went for a run, “to have something to Snapchat about.”

  • SAT

    Day 3

    9am to 6 pm

    It was Saturday and Luke and his partner, Jay, needed to go shopping for a birthday gift for Jay’s brother. Unfortunately, the gift Jay wanted to buy was something he couldn't track down-- a book about Simpson’s art. It was very interesting to watch the two of them look online and call shop after shop, trying to figure out who might stock the book that day. If only they had planned ahead to buy it on eBay!

Lowell regularly scrolls back through his own Instagram photos, to remember happy things. “Here is a photo of the bananas I grew last month. They were gorgeous.”

“I try to be selective about what I Instagram because it catalogs your photos. SnapChat I’ll do all day! I SnapChat too much. I go for walks to find fun things to SnapChat, that’s my pastime.”

“Hey, Kyle, look at Carl’s photo on Instagram. Is that the shirt I just bought?”

He turns to me and says,

“I buy online, otherwise you find out through Instagram that someone has the same shirt as you!"

“Every time it says, ‘Do you want to allow push notifications?’ I’m like, ‘No.!’ It’s annoying! You don’t want to be notified about everything. It has a little number, on top of an app, it’s there. That’s all you need. You don’t need a notification that comes up and says, ‘Someone has SnapChatted you’…Facebook and Messages, that’s all I get [pushed]. I guess I do get SnapChat too, I need to turn that off. I’m always checking it anyway. But there is a time and place for those things.”

“I don’t know why I love my horoscope app, but I check it every morning. I don’t really believe in those things... Though I have been to a psychic. I’m going to have to update the app to get tomorrow’s horoscope... I love reading it.”

“When I’m shopping online I do a lot on my laptop, but I have all the apps on my phone. I don’t buy on my phone, I only buy on my laptop, because I don’t think it’s… It seems more secure, but I don’t know why. You can’t see the product as well. If you are buying clothes you have to look at it on the laptop, because you might see some detail you didn’t know was there… like, ‘Oh, there are tabs on the shoulders… I don’t like that.”

On Facebook he clicks through on celebrity news, TV news, including the Wheel of Fortune clip,

“I’m addicted to watching these sorts of things, game show news, especially American ones, I don’t know why... Oh my god, that’s amazing!”

Notifications are unnecessary for apps that get checked addictively.

MEET LOWELL'S PATNER, KYLE

I hate waiting for things I buy online, but it’s much cheaper. I’ll often waste a whole day looking for something in every store before I finally order it online.

Sydney | Avid clothing shopper, music collector

Lowell’s partner and roommate. 25 years old, part-time student, part-time clothing store associate.

Understands how to look for quality, so will pay more for well-made items. Loves to shop, but Lowell doesn’t, so at the mall he is careful to be fast and let Lowell choose the stores.

Uses eBay sometimes to find items for his music collection (David Bowie concert footage, Bjork albums from Japan) but distrusts the authenticity of items and the reliability of sellers.

How difficult is it to assess quality, fit, feel, and authenticity online?

“We have such limited selection here that you try to shop overseas just to have something different. Otherwise you’re at a festival, and oh look, we’re all wearing the same thing.”

“Now with ASOS, we start to expect free shipping. And they have really good sales. I usually get an email with 70% off. It’s the stuff they haven’t sold in England, but it’s still good.”

“We really like Urban Outfitters, but sometimes they won’t ship to Australia. But on Australia Day they ship for free. When we went on a shopping holiday to the US we brought back so much stuff! I got stopped in Customs because they couldn’t believe I was carrying in a record player. But it was a $100 more in Australia!”

“I want to buy my brother a book that I saw in a store last month. It’s his birthday today. But I don’t know the name of it. It has illustrations of the Simpsons, but it wasn’t about the Simpsons...Lowell, will you call and ask about it? They weren’t helpful when I called.”

Lowell says,

“OK, write down what you know about it.” But while Charles is writing, Lowell performs a Google search for the book, “Is this it?” Shows the image on his phone to Charles. “Yes, you found it! Thank you!”

“Now where are we going to find it today? Let’s start calling bookstores.”

At Dymock’s book store Lowell shows the clerk the book cover on his phone. She looks it up in her computer. “We don’t have it in stock, but we can order it from overseas supplier and have it in 1-2 weeks.”

“I love the demonstrations at T2, they will show you the intricacies of each tea. I got hooked on it, I learn each one. I can make a perfect chai latte now! I don’t even go out for them because they aren’t as good as mine!”

“I tried on those jeans because I thought I might be a size bigger. I just wanted to check. I’ll order them online from a different store to get a discount.”

“I’m addicted to watching these sorts of things, game show news, especially American ones, I don’t know why... Oh my god, that’s amazing!”

LUKE'S GROUP

Beth is Lowell’s friend from uni. She is a teacher who uses an iPad constantly throughout the day in the classroom. She doesn’t miss it when she’s home.

Hatti is an assistant in the music industry. She is familiar with Amazon because she often buys things for her bosses to be shipped to the US. She is jealous that shipping is too expensive for Australians to use Amazon.

Pamela is Lowell’s aunty. They talk every day, just to gossip. She loves her iPad and uses it to shop for concert tickets and clothes and to find recipes.

Is there a clear split in Tablet attitudes? Either it goes everywhere with me, or it never leaves the couch.

Beth

I’m using Alien Blue to read Reddit. I can’t go to the Reddit website. I can’t read it, it’s a foreign language to me because I’m so used to using the app. I would go and use the website only if I wanted to post content. But I haven’t posted anything in years.

“Instagram makes you look good. I don’t see a lot of my family as often as I used to since I moved away from home. I see photos of the babies, and that makes me happy. Plus you get to show everyone the fun things you are doing.”

“Social media is very self-indulgent.”

Hatti

She is an assistant in the music industry. She feels the pressure of bosses who work all hours and expect a response from her,

"The fact that I have access to my emails on my phone means that I should constantly be checking them and replying to them, so it makes me feel guilty when I don’t check it or update it.”

“I really love music and I feel like Spotify takes care of me by giving me access to any music I like, and recommendations, and let’s me take it anywhere. I have the paid-for one, I pay $14 a month to have this on my iPhone. I take care of it and it takes care of me.”

Pamela

She says that she uses fewer apps because she is older and doesn’t hear about them, “I’m old so I don’t use as many apps as these guys. I probably use my banking app the most.

“I do use Google a lot. Like recipes, I do that all the time. If there was an app for that I’d be using it all the time, because I’m always Googling recipes.”

“I buy tickets through my iPad because I have a better chance of getting through. Everyone else is likely to be on a computer, so I go through my iPad.”

Australians see bargain hunting as a sport.
